Elbow Bending Angle Prediction Based on Upper Arm sEMG Signa

Team Works on Course Digital Signal Processing in the forth-year Undergraduate

Accomplishments
  • Surface electromyography (sEMG) is a physiological electromyography signal, which can be used to control intelligent prostheses and bionic hands in rehabilitation medicine.
  • It also contains information such as muscle force and joint motion angle, and can be used to evaluate limb motion state. This project studied the relationship between upper arm sEMG signals and the bending angle when the elbow is bent.
  • Through simple Time-Frequency analysis, the nonlinear correlation between the upper arm sEMG signal and the elbow joint bending angle was established. In this project, four filters are used to design IIR filters (Butterworth, Chebyshev I, Chebyshev II, elliptic filter ), and stable sEMG signals and angle data with consistent sampling frequency are obtained by full-wave rectification and secondary sampling.
  • Then, the prediction model between sEMG and angle is established by BP neural network, and the angle prediction based on sEMG is realized. Finally, the mechanical structure model of elbow rehabilitation equipment is built, and the predictive model is used as the controller to control it autonomously.
